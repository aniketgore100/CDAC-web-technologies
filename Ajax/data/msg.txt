Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was a prominent Maratha warrior king and the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India during the 17th century. Here's a brief biography of Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj:

- **Birth and Early Life:** Shivaji was born on February 19, 1630, in the hill fort of Shivneri in the Pune district of Maharashtra, India. He was born to Shahaji Bhonsle, a Maratha general, and Jijabai. He was named Shivaji Bhonsle at birth.

- **Rise to Power:** Shivaji's father served under the Deccan Sultanates and the Mughals. However, after the capture of his father by the Adil Shahi Sultanate, Shivaji began to establish his own independent Maratha kingdom. He initiated a guerrilla warfare campaign against the oppressive rule of various sultanates.

- **Military Achievements:** Shivaji was a brilliant military strategist and tactician. He built a strong navy and established a network of forts along the western coast of India. His notable military campaigns include the capture of important forts like Torna, Raigad, and Pratapgad.

- **Coronation as Chatrapati:** In 1674, Shivaji crowned himself as the Chatrapati (emperor) of the Maratha Kingdom at Raigad Fort.

- **Administration:** Shivaji was not only a skilled warrior but also an able administrator. He established a council of ministers called the Ashtapradhan (Council of Eight). He laid the foundation for a well-organized and decentralized administrative system.

- **Religious Tolerance:** Shivaji was known for his religious tolerance and promoted a policy of sarvadharma samabhava, which treated people of all religions equally.

- **Legacy:** Shivaji's rule had a lasting impact on the Indian subcontinent. He is celebrated as a hero of the Maratha people and a symbol of regional pride. His naval and military strategies influenced later Indian rulers and European colonial powers.

- **Death:** Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj died on April 3, 1680, at Raigad Fort. His legacy lived on through the Maratha Empire and its expansion under subsequent rulers.

Shivaji Maharaj's life and legacy continue to be a source of inspiration for people in Maharashtra and across India. His contributions to the Indian subcontinent, both as a military leader and an administrator, have made him a revered figure in Indian history.
